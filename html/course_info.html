Year 1: Building a Foundation

The first year focuses on building a strong foundation in ICT. Students are introduced to the basics of information and communication technology, including hardware, software, and networking. They learn how computers and devices work, how to set them up, and how to troubleshoot common technical issues. This includes studying the components of a computer (such as CPUs, RAM, and storage), understanding operating systems, and exploring how software interacts with hardware.


Key Subjects:

  • CCNA
  • IT Essentials
  • Elektriciteitsleer
  • Digitaal techniek
  • Microsoft Office

In the first year, you will work in teams on various projects designed to help you develop essential skills. While completing tasks, you will follow the guidelines outlined in a KD (Kwalificatiedossier). This experience will serve as excellent preparation for using the KD during your internships in the second year.


Duration

  • 3 semesters

Year 4: final year and graduation

In the final year, students focus on their chosen specialization, such as software development, or network engineering. They will also get ready to take the exams around december to determine if they move on to the internships of the next year.


Key Subjects

  • UML
  • Domotica
  • Projectmanagement
  • Distributed App
  • Microsoft Office

As explained earlier, students take their exams in December to demonstrate their knowledge and skills, which allows them to move forward to internships in the following year

During their internships, students work closely with experienced professionals in the IT industry, gaining hands-on experience and insight into day-to-day operations. In addition to their regular duties, students undertake a large-scale graduation project, which is often designed to solve a real problem or meet a specific need for the company they are interning with. These projects require them to integrate their technical skills, creativity, and problem-solving abilities to deliver a high-quality solution.


Duration

Exams in December, 1 full semester and an internship
